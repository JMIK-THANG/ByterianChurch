About Us

At Emmanuel Presbyterian Church, our ultimate goal to lead individuals to experience the mercy and grace of Jesus Christ, ultimately guiding them towards eternal life. We understand that stepping into a new church environment can be intimidating, which is why we strive to create a welcoming atmosphere where everyone feels like they truly belong. No one is without flaws, but through God's grace, we are all accepted just as we are. EPC embraces growth and transformation, recognizing that each new member brings something unique to our community. Join us and witness the love of Jesus in action as we support you in your journey of faith and spiritual growth.

Pastor Lal Kawl Tir

Pastor Tir is a second-generation member of the Presbyterian Church of Myanmar, following the footsteps of his grandfather, a member and ordained elder of the church. He embraced Jesus as his personal savior in 5, committing his life to faithfully walking in the path of Christ. From a young age, Pastor Tir pursued his divine calling, immersing himself in seminary and theological studies.
At just 15 years old in 1999, he embarked on his journey at a Biblical seminary, ultimately earning a Bachelor of Theology from Bethel Theological Seminary (BTS) in 2006. Concurrently, through dedicated distance learning, he also obtained a Bachelor of Science from Mandalay (Secular College). In 2015, Pastor Tir achieved his Master in Divinity from Tahan Theological College through distance learning.
Driven by a fervent zeal to spread the gospel, Pastor Tir passionately engages in mission work, fearlessly preaching in unreached territories. Committed to community development, he selflessly imparts English classes during the summer months. In March 2011, Pastor Tir united in marriage with Lalven Thuang, their union blessed with three children—Lal Biak Muan, Siang Rin Tluang, and Zir Sang Lian, with another little one on the way.
In 2012, as a refugee in the United States, Pastor Tir answered the call to minister at the Chin Christian Presbyterian Church of Indiana (CCPC), receiving ordination in 2018. With unwavering dedication, he embraces his divine calling, resolved to serve in ministry until the end of his days.
